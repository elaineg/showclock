# Showclock
Purpose: a paste-and-go run-of-show timer for workshop facilitators and event MCs that shows live ahead/behind drift and auto-reflows the clock times of remaining agenda items.
Problem: A workshop facilitator, trainer, or event MC runs multi-segment sessions weekly and does mental math mid-session ("we started Q&A 7 minutes late — what do I cut?").
Beats alternative: stagetimer.io free tier (verified: no session-length cap, but max 3 timers per room — a 6–12 item agenda needs Pro at $210/yr), Ontime (free, open source, genuinely capable, but a desktop/self-hosted install aimed at broadcast — real friction for a casual facilitator on a phone podium), or a spreadsheet of start times (free, stale the moment one segment runs long). Closest free no-signup threat is MeetingTimer.eu (re-verified 2026-06-12): multi-item agenda with auto-advance and a view-only link, but NO computed clock times, NO ahead/behind drift, NO auto-reflow of remaining items, and manual item-by-item entry only — drift + reflow + paste-to-parse + wall-clock times are exactly what this app adds.

Core flows:
1. Paste, then edit in place: paste a free-text agenda (one item per line; parser tolerates "Intro 10", "Intro - 10 min", and "10 Intro"), pick a start time (defaults to the next 5-minute mark), and instantly see the parsed rundown — item name, duration, and planned wall-clock start time for every item, recomputed live as the text or start time changes. Unparseable lines are flagged inline without breaking the rest. The parsed rundown rows are then directly EDITABLE in place (before Start): click/tab a row's title or duration to edit (commit on blur/Enter), use per-row ↑/↓ to reorder and × to delete, "+ Add item" to append, and every change reflows all planned clock times live. Structured edits are authoritative — the edited rundown is what Start, the share URL, and the presenter view all use; the paste box stays the bulk-replace entry path and stays in sync. "Copy rundown as text" emits the current rundown as paste-compatible lines that round-trip back into the box.
2. Presenter view with drift + reflow + stage cues: hit Start to enter a large-type presenter view showing the current item, its countdown, a signed drift badge ("on time", "N min ahead", "N min behind"), and the remaining items with BOTH planned and projected wall-clock start times. Pressing Next records the actual start of the following item; drift = actual minus planned start (and keeps growing live once the current item overruns its duration), and all remaining projected times reflow by the current drift. A Back control undoes an accidental Next. STAGE CUES make the current item readable from across a room: a full-width color band wraps the current-item block — GREEN when ample time remains, AMBER in the final stretch (last max(60s, 20% of the item's duration)), RED the instant the countdown passes 0:00, with the red band gently pulsing on overrun (static on prefers-reduced-motion). An opt-in, default-OFF labeled "Sound" toggle (visible on cold load) arms a WebAudio beep that fires at item time-up and once on sustained overrun; the toggle press is the gesture that arms audio, and the preference is per-device (localStorage), not in the URL.
3. Share URL for co-facilitators: agenda, start timestamp, and per-item actual-start marks are all encoded in the URL (updated on every Start/Next), so copying the address into another tab or device shows a read-only view — same current item, countdown, drift, reflowed times, AND the same green/amber/red stage-cue band (color is derived from timing the viewer already has), derived entirely client-side with no controls and no backend. The host's sound preference is NOT shared (it's a per-device output choice); each viewer arms their own. Reloading the presenter URL restores the running session.

Success checks:
- Pasting "Intro 10\nDemo - 20 min\n15 Q&A" shows three rows named Intro, Demo, Q&A with durations 10, 20, 15 and cumulative planned clock times (with a 9:00 AM start: 9:00, 9:10, 9:30) before Start is pressed.
- Adding a line "just some words" flags that line as unparsed while the other rows keep their computed clock times.
- Changing the start time from 9:00 to 9:30 immediately shifts every planned clock time by 30 minutes.
- Editing the parsed rundown in place: with the agenda above and a 9:00 start, changing the Demo item's duration from 20 to 40 (click the duration, type 40, press Enter) reflows every later planned clock time live (Q&A moves from 9:30 to 9:50) with no re-paste.
- Clicking the up-arrow (↑) on the 2nd item swaps it with the 1st item and the planned clock-time column updates so the new order's cumulative times are correct; the down-arrow (↓) does the reverse. The ↑ on the first row and ↓ on the last row are disabled (inert, still visible).
- Clicking "+ Add item" appends a new editable row whose duration, once set, extends the planned clock times; clicking a row's × removes it and all later clock times reflow up. Deleting every item disables Start with an "add at least one item" hint.
- "Copy rundown as text" produces plaintext lines (e.g. "Intro 10") that, pasted back into the box, reproduce the identical rundown (same names, durations, order, and computed clock times).
- Edits made before Start round-trip: after editing/reordering, clicking Start shows the presenter view in the edited order with the edited durations, and copying the share URL opens a read-only view of that same edited rundown (a wrong recomputed clock time after any edit is a P0).
- At 375px width every row's ↑ ↓ × controls and the "+ Add item" / "Copy rundown as text" buttons render and are operable by tap (≥40px targets), with no hover-only reveal and no drag required.
- Clicking Start shows a presenter view with "Intro" as the current item, a countdown ticking down from 10:00, and a drift badge reading on time / 0 min.
- Clicking Next about 9 minutes early (i.e. immediately after Start on the agenda above) shows a drift badge of "9 min ahead" (±1 min) and remaining items whose projected clock times are ~9 minutes earlier than their still-visible planned times.
- Letting the current item run past its duration flips the drift badge to "behind" and increases it as time passes, with remaining projected times shifting later accordingly.
- Copying the presenter URL into a second browser tab (or incognito window) shows the same current item, drift, and reflowed times with no Start/Next/Back controls visible.
- Reloading the presenter tab mid-session restores the same current item and drift from the URL alone (works with the network tab showing no API calls for state).
- Stage-cue color band — with a 1-minute item the current-item band is AMBER from Start (amber window = max(60s, 12s) = full minute), turns RED the instant the countdown passes 0:00, and the red band visibly pulses; with a 10-minute item the band is GREEN at start, turns AMBER inside the final ~2:00, and RED past 0:00. The band is full-width and bold (not just a tinted number), and is the dominant block under the drift badge.
- Sound toggle — the presenter view shows a clearly-labeled sound control reading "Sound: Off" on cold load (default OFF), discoverable without opening any menu. Switching it On plays a brief confirmation blip; thereafter a WebAudio beep fires the instant the current item passes 0:00, with one reminder beep ~30s into overrun. With Sound Off, no beep ever fires.
- Reduced-motion — with the OS "reduce motion" setting on, an overrunning item shows a solid (non-pulsing) red band; color alone still reads as RED.
- Read-only share band — opening the read-only share URL of a session whose current item is overrunning shows the same RED (pulsing) band and over-time countdown, with no Start/Next/Back/Sound-arming controls beyond the viewer's own sound toggle.

Out of scope:
- No accounts, login, or saved rundown library; no backend or database — all state lives in the URL.
- No live sync server: the co-facilitator view reflects the URL it was opened with (refresh/re-copy to update), not a real-time socket feed.
- PRE-START editing IS in scope: inline edit of title/duration, add/delete, reorder (↑/↓), and "Copy rundown as text" plaintext export all happen in the planning view before Start. What remains OUT of scope is editing the agenda MID-SHOW (beyond Back/Next) — no pause, no per-item time adjustments, no reorder once a show is running.
- No speaker messages or custom themes. Plaintext "Copy rundown as text" export (paste-compatible lines) IS in scope; CSV/ICS FILE import-export remains out of scope. (Audio is also IN scope as a single opt-in, default-off WebAudio cue beep — no uploaded sounds, no per-tone customization.)
- No sub-minute drift precision guarantees; drift is displayed in whole minutes.
