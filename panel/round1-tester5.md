# Dana — Demand-gen marketer, MacBook + phone

## Clarity — Yes
Within five seconds I got it. The H1 "Showclock" plus the subhead "Paste your run-of-show,
hit Start, and always know if you're ahead or behind. No accounts — the whole session lives
in the URL." told me precisely what it is and that it's for live presenters. The placeholder
agenda ("Intro 10", "Demo - 20 min", "15 Q&A") instantly clicked with how I think about my
webinar segments. "No accounts" is a real plus — I'm not creating a login for a 50-minute
webinar. I'd explain it to a peer in one line: "Paste your webinar rundown, it shows a giant
clock and tells you if you're behind so you can trim Q&A."

## Value — Yes
Today I do this with a Notion table of segment times and a phone stopwatch, doing mental math
mid-demo to figure out if I can afford the full Q&A. That math is exactly what kills me when
I'm presenting. Showclock does the math for me: it parses messy lines ("Intro - 5 min",
"15 Q&A", "Wrap-up 5") without me reformatting, shows a big countdown, and — the part that
won me — a rundown that recalculates the PROJECTED end time and every segment's new clock time
live. When I forced a behind state, "12 min behind" went orange/amber and the projected times
turned orange too; the ahead state was a calm blue pill. That is the glanceable signal I want
on a second screen. The "Copy view-only link" worked (it copied the full URL) so I could hand
my co-host a read-only timer without giving them edit access. This genuinely saves me effort
versus my Notion + stopwatch ritual.

## Advocacy — 8/10
I'd screenshot this into the team channel. It's fast, it's free, no signup, and it nails the
one job. What holds it back from a 9–10: the landing page makes me work before I see the
payoff. The behind/ahead screen — the thing I'd actually recommend — is hidden behind pasting
an agenda and clicking Start. A one-tap "see a live example" would have sold me in the first
scroll instead of the second screen. Smaller nit: in the behind state the giant clock still
counts the current segment ("4:59") while the pill says "12 min behind" — for a beat I wasn't
sure which number to trust at a glance; the pill is dominant enough that I trust it, but I'd
want the cumulative drift to be the hero number. Neither is a dealbreaker. I'd use this for
next month's webinar.
