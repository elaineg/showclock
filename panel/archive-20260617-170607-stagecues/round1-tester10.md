# Sam — Product Manager (mobile, between meetings)

## Clarity — Yes
Landed on my phone and within ~5 seconds I got it: big "Showclock" title, then "Paste your run-of-show, hit Start, and always know if you're ahead or behind. No accounts — the whole session lives in the URL." That subhead did all the work. The pre-filled example agenda ("Welcome 5", "Intro - 10 min", "15 Q&A") instantly told me what to type and that it's forgiving about format. "No accounts" + "lives in the URL" is the exact reassurance I need before I'll trust a tool in front of a room. This is for someone running a timed session — that's me every sprint planning.

## Value — Yes
Today I run sprint planning and roadmap workshops off a Notion agenda with times typed next to each item, and I eyeball the wall clock while doing math in my head ("we blew 8 min on grooming, so pointing now ends at..."). I get it wrong, or I look disorganized stalling to recalc. Showclock does exactly the thing I do badly: I pasted my real agenda (Sprint goal review, grooming, pointing, etc.), hit Start, and got a presenter view with a "planned → projected" rundown. I deliberately set it running behind and the whole rundown reflowed — every downstream item's projected time jumped later and turned amber, "projected end 10:49 PM" updated, and an amber "3 min behind" badge appeared. That is the moment that makes the room trust the schedule. No re-entry, no spreadsheet, paste-and-go. The share link is the whole state encoded in the URL, so I can drop a read-only link to my co-facilitator in Slack. That's a genuine time and credibility win over my Notion-plus-mental-math habit.

## Advocacy — 8
I'd actually bring this up in our PM channel next time someone complains about workshops running over. Two things keep it off a 9/10:
1. I clicked "Copy view-only link" and nothing visibly confirmed — no "Copied!" flip, no toast. In a live session I'll click it twice unsure it worked. The link DID copy (verified), but I shouldn't have to wonder.
2. The huge per-item countdown and the "X min behind" badge didn't always feel in sync at a glance — the giant clock dominates the screen, and in a workshop I want the AHEAD/BEHIND number to be the hero, since that's what I announce to the room. Right now the minutes-behind badge is secondary to a clock that's less actionable.

Neither is a dealbreaker. The core promise — paste, share, watch it reflow — delivered, on mobile, with zero setup. That alone earns the recommend.
